# **CASE STUDY: UPI Fraud**

UPI fraud is a common type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into transferring money through digital payment apps. This usually happens through fake calls, messages, or links that appear to be from banks or trusted services.

In a typical scenario, a student receives a message claiming that they have won a reward or need to verify their account. The message includes a link or a request to accept a payment request. The attacker may also call the victim pretending to be a bank representative and ask them to share OTPs or PINs. Once the victim shares this information or approves the request, money is instantly transferred from their account.

Students and young adults are often targeted because they frequently use UPI apps and may not be fully aware of such scams. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and sometimes compromise of personal information.

This type of fraud highlights the importance of awareness and caution while using digital payment systems. Understanding how these scams work can help users avoid becoming victims and protect their financial information.
